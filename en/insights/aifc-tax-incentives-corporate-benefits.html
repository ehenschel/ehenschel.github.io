AIFC Tax Incentives and Corporate Benefits: A Complete Overview

Comprehensive analysis of the Astana International Financial Centre framework, including corporate income tax exemptions and special legal privileges for residents.

November 30, 2025 · 7 min read · MinTax Group Legal Advisory

What This Article Will Cover

The Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC) offers one of the most attractive tax and legal regimes in Central Asia. Established in 2018, the AIFC operates under English common law principles with its own court system, providing a unique business environment within Kazakhstan.

AIFC participants benefit from a comprehensive package of tax incentives that extend through 2066: exemption from corporate income tax, individual income tax on dividends, and property tax. The Centre also provides simplified foreign currency operations, protection under English law, and access to specialized dispute resolution through the AIFC Court.

For companies considering registration, understanding the eligibility criteria, ongoing compliance requirements, and practical implications of operating within the AIFC framework is essential for making an informed decision.

Key Points to Be Covered

  • AIFC legal framework and governance structure
  • Tax incentive package: CIT, PIT, property tax exemptions through 2066
  • Eligibility criteria and registration process
  • Permitted activities within the AIFC
  • Regulatory requirements and ongoing compliance
  • Comparison with other financial centres (DIFC, ADGM)
  • Practical considerations for companies considering AIFC registration
