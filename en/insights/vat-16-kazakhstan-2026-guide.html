VAT 16% in Kazakhstan 2026: A Practical Guide for Businesses

Kazakhstan raised its VAT rate from 12% to 16% on January 1, 2026. Here is what every business needs to know to prepare and stay compliant.

[Article Featured Image]

Effective January 1, 2026, Kazakhstan implemented one of its most significant tax reforms — raising the standard VAT rate from 12% to 16%. This 33% increase affects virtually every business operating in the country.

What Changed

The increase is part of Kazakhstan's fiscal consolidation strategy, aimed at funding infrastructure, social programs, and economic diversification.

Key Facts

  • New rate: 16% standard VAT (up from 12%)
  • Effective date: January 1, 2026
  • Export rate: 0% unchanged
  • Exempt services: Financial, medical, educational remain exempt
  • Registration threshold: Annual turnover exceeding 20,000 MRP (approximately 78.6M tenge in 2026)

Impact on Business

The higher rate has immediate implications for pricing, cash flow, and compliance procedures.

Pricing

In B2B transactions, VAT is fully recoverable. In B2C — retail, hospitality, services — businesses must decide whether to absorb the 4% difference or pass it to consumers.

Cash Flow

Larger VAT amounts must be remitted on schedule. Review cash flow forecasts and ensure liquidity for monthly or quarterly payments.

Compliance Checklist

  1. Update accounting software and ERP systems to 16%
  2. Reconfigure electronic invoices in IS ESF
  3. Update all price lists and commercial proposals
  4. Train accounting staff on the new rates
  5. Recalculate advance payments and tax reserves
  6. Review contracts with long-term pricing provisions

Transitional Provisions

Goods shipped before January 1, 2026 but invoiced after are subject to 12%, provided shipping documents predate the effective date. Services follow the same principle.

Carefully document all transactions spanning the January 1 transition to avoid disputes during future audits.
