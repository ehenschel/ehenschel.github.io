Transfer Pricing in the CIS: Documentation Requirements and Risk Management

Understanding Kazakhstan's transfer pricing regulations, OECD guidelines, and strategies to minimize tax audit risks in cross-border transactions.

December 28, 2025 · 10 min read · MinTax Group Tax Advisory

What This Article Will Cover

Transfer pricing remains one of the highest-risk areas for tax audits in Kazakhstan and across the CIS region. With tax authorities increasingly sophisticated in their analysis of intercompany transactions, proper documentation and proactive risk management are essential.

Kazakhstan's transfer pricing rules align broadly with OECD guidelines but contain important local variations. The threshold for mandatory documentation, the acceptable methods for benchmarking, and the penalties for non-compliance have all been updated under the new Tax Code.

For companies with cross-border operations — particularly in oil and gas, mining, and financial services — transfer pricing compliance is not optional. Tax authorities actively scrutinize related-party transactions, and the burden of proof falls on the taxpayer.

Key Points to Be Covered

  • Kazakhstan's transfer pricing legal framework under the new Tax Code
  • OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines and local adaptations
  • Documentation requirements: who must prepare, what to include, deadlines
  • Benchmarking methodologies accepted by Kazakh tax authorities
  • Common audit triggers and high-risk transaction types
  • Cross-border considerations within the CIS and EAEU
  • Dispute resolution and advance pricing agreements
