March 1 Deadline: Choosing Your Tax Regime Under the New Code

Every entrepreneur who operated under a special tax regime in 2025 must notify the tax authority of their chosen regime by March 1, 2026. Missing this deadline means automatic deregistration — retroactive to January 1.

February 18, 2026 · 5 min read · MinTax Group Tax Advisory

What Happened

Kazakhstan's new Tax Code, effective January 1, 2026, consolidated seven special tax regimes into three. The patent-based regime, the special mobile application regime, the retail tax, and several other categories were eliminated. In their place, the Code establishes three streamlined options: self-employment, simplified declaration, and peasant/farm household.

The transitional provisions require every existing taxpayer who was registered under any of the discontinued regimes to actively choose one of the three remaining options and notify the tax authority. This is not automatic. If you do nothing, the tax authority will do it for you — and the result is deregistration.

What happens if you miss the deadline

Taxpayers who applied the patent-based regime or the special mobile application regime in 2025, and who have not submitted a notification by March 1, 2026, will be automatically deregistered as individual entrepreneurs. The deregistration date will be recorded as January 1, 2026 — meaning you will be treated as if you were never registered this year. Any business activity conducted without registration may carry administrative penalties.

The Three Regimes Available

1

Self-Employed Regime

For individuals working independently with no employees. Monthly income limit: 340 MCI (~KZT 1.47M). You pay only social contributions — no individual income tax. All interactions with the tax authority happen through a dedicated mobile app, including receipt generation and reporting. This regime is best suited for freelancers, sole practitioners, and gig workers with modest, regular income.

2

Simplified Declaration

The primary regime for small and medium business. Annual income limit: 600,000 MCI (~KZT 2.6B). Combined CIT/IIT rate: 4% (local maslikhats may adjust by up to 50%). No social tax. No limit on employee count. You may operate branches. Reporting via Form 910.00, filed semi-annually. Critical: This regime cannot be combined with VAT payer status under the new Code. If your turnover exceeds the VAT threshold (10,000 MCI, ~KZT 43.25M), you must choose between the simplified regime (no VAT, 4% rate) and the general regime (full VAT compliance with input recovery).

3

Peasant & Farm Household

Exclusively for agricultural producers. Replaces the former unified land tax. IIT rate: 0.5%, payable annually. No VAT obligation except on imports and payments to non-residents. Transition for existing farm households will be handled automatically by tax authorities by March 1, 2026 — no notification is required from the taxpayer.

How to Submit Your Notification

The notification must be submitted through the Taxpayer Cabinet (cabinet.salyk.kz) or through the eGov.kz portal. The form requires you to indicate which regime you are electing, confirm that you meet the eligibility conditions, and specify the date of application (which should be January 1, 2026 for existing entrepreneurs).

For businesses transitioning from the patent or mobile app regime to simplified declaration: Submit the notification of applied tax regime before March 1, 2026. The starting date of the regime will be considered January 1, 2026, regardless of when in February you file. There is no advantage to waiting — file now.

The VAT Decision

For many businesses, the March 1 deadline is intertwined with a separate strategic question: should you be a VAT payer?

Under the old Code, it was possible to operate on a simplified regime while also being registered for VAT. Under the new Code, this combination is expressly prohibited. If your annual turnover exceeds 10,000 MCI (approximately KZT 43.25 million) and you need to be a VAT payer, you must transition to the general taxation procedure — which means CIT at 20%, full VAT compliance, and standard reporting requirements.

Conversely, if you wish to retain the simplified declaration regime (4% combined rate, semi-annual reporting), you must deregister as a VAT payer if you are currently registered. This decision has cascading effects on pricing, supplier relationships, and working capital. It is not a decision to make at the last minute.

What MinTax Recommends

The process itself takes minutes, but choosing the right regime requires understanding how the VAT threshold, your actual revenue trajectory, and the new rates interact with your specific business model.
